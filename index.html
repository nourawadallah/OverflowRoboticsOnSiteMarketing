Technical Gallery

Meet Encore

Encore — Full Vehicle
Carbon fibre frame, 45×35×35 cm, 9 kg. Built for extreme cold-water and under-ice operations.
Dual Front Grippers
Double-acting pneumatic pistons for connector tasks, cap removal, power connector installation, and sensor connection.
Four-fin gripper
Four-Fin Gripper
Designed specifically for the anode task.
Tilted Arms
Used to collect O-Balls. Arms tilt to match negative buoyancy, keeping balls stable during retrieval.
Flexible Mechanism
One-way entrance mechanism driven by a linear actuator, used to connect to the buoy anchor via fishing-rod rope.
Airbag System
Inflates on demand to increase buoyancy and carry heavy loads for pot retrieval tasks.
Go-BGC Float
Non-ROV double-syringe buoyancy system for autonomous under-ice profiling, communicating via Bluetooth with the station.
Electronics interior
Electronics Interior
Three custom PCBs for signal and power distribution, with a Jetson Orin Nano Super running AI vision in real time.

Community & Outreach

Building Engineers,
Not Just Robots

Overflow Robotics runs a structured internal mentoring programme. Our Grade 12 engineers directly mentor Grade 9, 10, and 11 members across all three divisions — mechanical, electrical, and software. This season, junior members completed their first full engineering build cycle under senior guidance.

Beyond internal mentoring, Overflow Robotics actively works to extend the skills and passion we develop in-house to the wider community — running workshops, school visits, and open demonstrations to show students across Alexandria what engineering looks like up close.
